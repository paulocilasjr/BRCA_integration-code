### Stratified k-fold cross-validation

We used stratified k-fold cross-validation to evaluate the performance of the functional-evidence classification framework while preserving the proportion of pathogenic and benign reference variants across folds. Reference labels were assigned from the established T6 classification categories: variants with T6 values of 4, 5 or 4;5 were treated as pathogenic reference variants, and variants with T6 values of 1, 2 or 1;2 were treated as benign reference variants. BRCA1 start-codon variants p.M1I, p.M1K, p.M1R, p.M1T and p.M1V were excluded from reference-label assignment for this analysis. Variants were included when they had an eligible reference label and at least one available assay-track result among the metadata-listed assay tracks. BRCA1 variants were partitioned into ten folds and BRCA2 variants into five folds.

Fold allocation was performed using StratifiedKFold with shuffling enabled and a fixed random seed of 42. At each iteration, assay-specific evidence strengths were derived exclusively from the training folds. For each assay track, training-set reference variants were used to estimate the odds of pathogenicity associated with functional-impact and normal-function assay results; these odds were then mapped to the fixed ACMG BS3/PS3 evidence-strength categories. The resulting assay-specific evidence assignments were applied to the held-out fold, ensuring that held-out variants did not contribute to the training-fold evidence-strength estimates.

Held-out variants were classified by combining the evidence generated across available assay tracks. Assay-track results supporting normal function contributed BS3 evidence, results supporting functional impact contributed PS3 evidence, and hypomorphic results were labeled separately. Variants with BS3 evidence and no PS3 evidence were assigned a benign functional-evidence call, and variants with PS3 evidence and no BS3 evidence were assigned a pathogenic functional-evidence call. Variants with only indeterminate or hypomorphic evidence were treated as no-calls. For mixed BS3 and PS3 evidence, the variant was assigned to the direction supported by at least a three-fold excess of evidence counts; otherwise the result was treated as a no-call.

Performance was evaluated in each fold using sensitivity, specificity and the Matthews correlation coefficient. Ninety-five percent confidence intervals for sensitivity and specificity were calculated using the Wilson score method. No-call variants were reported separately as counts and proportions and were not included in the sensitivity, specificity or Matthews correlation coefficient denominators. Training and test class counts were recorded for each fold to confirm preservation of the pathogenic-to-benign distribution.

Running the current Sup Table 11 code produces 135 pathogenic and 211 benign eligible BRCA1 reference variants across the stratified folds, and 19 pathogenic and 157 benign eligible BRCA2 reference variants. The average fold-level performance reported in Sup Table 11 is sensitivity 0.98, specificity 0.99, no-call rate 0.11 and Matthews correlation coefficient 0.97 for BRCA1; and sensitivity 1.00, specificity 0.97, no-call rate 0.10 and Matthews correlation coefficient 0.80 for BRCA2.
